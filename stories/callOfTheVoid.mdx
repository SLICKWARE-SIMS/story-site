## Call of the Void

I could feel it in my bones. I always thought 'the call of the void' referred to a mysterious, sudden, dangerous motivator from the depths of our own psyche. A deep-seated desire that bubbled up from within and manifested in an ache in every limb, a tingling in your fingers. A need to Do Something Drastic. A selfish impulse to leap into the unknown.

Now I know it’s something else.


An afternoon broadcast crackled. The history of a distant station orbiting a dead planet, what had happened and what remained. Something was waiting and, from my home system, it was straight down. I believed that the decision I made that evening to leave home with nothing and cram myself into the first passing ship capable of the journey with space enough to contort my weak body into was wholly my own. I leapt into the void. In reality, Indra's net had been cast and I was, as all things are, ensnared in it, every gleaming eye reflecting and refracting and staring unblinking at me as I plummet towards Trinculo, Prospero's Dream circling to catch me before I burst into flames and disintegrate.


I survived the fall. I waited for my revelation. I thought that ache would subside but it returned the moment I set foot on the station and intensified each passing moment. It drives me to live here, to thrive here, to take risks and survive another day by the skin of my teeth instead of waiting to die on a rock a trillion miles above. I have descended into depths and found myself like so many denizens before and around me. It isn't just in my bones anymore, it ripples through my skin and thrums through my racing blood. It echoes through my skull. I can feel it in the gel of my eyes and it forces them open in the dark. I can place my hand on a window and feel the station reverberate with it. There are days when I can hear it, too, sorrowful and resonant, crying out for company. I have joined its chorus.

The call of the void is whalesong, and it has invited us to Dream.
